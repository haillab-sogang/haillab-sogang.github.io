About

Hi. I am a Ph.D. student at KAIST School of Computing, advised by Alice Oh.

My research focuses on developing technology that helps people socialize and collaborate better with their co-learners and be more engaged in online programming classes. It is tricky to provide social presence from other learners to online learners, but a lack of social presence in learning can cause learner frustration, anxiety, and less engagement. I have been mainly working on developing an interactive learning environment for online programming classes, and providing social presence to the online learners with visual cues from their co-learners.

I have also been interested in discovering latent patterns from unstructured data collected from Internet and helping people to socialize and collaborate better in online; my previous research in this topic includes predicting political stance of Twitter users, analyzing relationships between Twitter users in different demographic and political groups, or measuring conflicts between users from different cultures in Wikipedia articles.

I am currently developing a web-based online programming class that supports resource-efficient co-learner screen sharing feature in the working environment.
